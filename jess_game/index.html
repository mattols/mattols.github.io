Final Challenge: Write a SMART Goal

Based on the following scenario, write a SMART goal:

Emily is a 3rd grader struggling with math fluency. She can solve 10 addition problems in 2 minutes, but she needs to reach 20 problems by the end of the semester.

Instructions: Write a SMART goal based on the following criteria:

  • S: Specific — Clearly describe the student's goal.
  • M: Measurable — Include how the progress will be measured.
  • A: Achievable — Ensure it's attainable based on the student's current abilities.
  • R: Relevant — Relate the goal to the student's needs.
  • T: Time-bound — Set a specific timeframe for achievement (e.g., by the end of the semester).

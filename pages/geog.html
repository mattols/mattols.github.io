Program Learning Objectives

Geography is an integrative scholarly discipline analyzing complex environmental and social dynamics from local to global scales. Through an approach spanning the sciences and humanities, geography reveals insights on how communities both influence and are impacted by their landscapes.

Unifying the geographic perspective are the concepts of “place” and “space” and the intricate interactions between people, culture, politics, economics, land, water, plants and wildlife over time. Geographers employ spatial analysis and mapping applications such as geographic information systems (GIS) to discern revealing patterns and trends. They leverage these technologies alongside statistical data to explain causal mechanisms and guide impactful policies and decisions.

Through examining philosophical questions about sustainable development, social justice, and humanity’s bond within an interconnected world, the study of geography prepares students for building thriving, resilient places for the future.

Upon successful program completion, students will be able to

  1. Synthesize the interconnections between physical and human systems, environment and society, and the applications of geography across spatial and temporal scales.
  2. Interpret quantitative and qualitative geographic data using geospatial technologies.
  3. Evaluate causes, consequences, and possible solutions to relevant, persistent, contemporary, and emerging global issues using an interdisciplinary, scientific perspective.
  4. Communicate scientific data and geographic topics through technical and creative writing, the production of figures, maps, and public and scientific presentations.
  5. Collaborate with others through discussion, team problem solving, and group research to build interpersonal and leadership skills.

Students are encouraged to tailor their degree through pathways which provide guidance toward a particular career path. Each pathway is designed to provide student with the necessary skills and experience to succeed in their desired field of study.
